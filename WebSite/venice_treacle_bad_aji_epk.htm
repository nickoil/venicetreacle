Short Bio

Venice Treacle craft their own brand of crooked, melodic psych-punk known as Quank - a sound full of rhythmic left turns, warped edges, and unexpected clarity. Their music blends wonky grooves with bright melodic bursts, creating songs that feel at once disorienting and satisfyingly appropriate. Onstage, the band push their quirks even further, building a reputation for electric, unpredictable performances that is steadily spreading beyond their West Country origins.

About “Bad Aji”

Bad Aji takes inspiration from legendary Go master Lee Sedol and his historic defeat in 2016 by an intelligent computer program, DeepMind's AlphaGo. In one game, the machine made a move that no human would ever consider - one which was first dismissed as a mistake. But deeper analysis revealed the brilliance of the shoulder hit, and that it was a turning point for both the match and for what AI could do.

Venice Treacle reinterpret the confrontation through the Quank aesthetic: wonky rhythmic jolts and melodic fragments mirror the shifting dynamics of Sedol’s battle, an early engagement in an enduring war which the algorithms seem to be winning. The band hasten to reassure that the track was composed, performed and mixed entirely by humans - a recipe of their own concoction with just a dash of AI sauce in the mastering process.
